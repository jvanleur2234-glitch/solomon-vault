**Core Insight:** Companies will PAY to NOT see your data.

Think about it:

- Facebook makes $130B+/year the same way

**Your privacy is worth more to others than it is to you.**

## HOW IT WORKS

**"Privacy Futures" — like a market, but for opt-out of surveillance.**

### Step 1: The Privacy Score (Like a Credit Score, But Inverted)

- Your AI tracks what you share vs. what you keep private
- High score = you consistently choose local processing over cloud
- Companies can see your score WITHOUT seeing your data
- Score proves you're the type of person who values privacy
- People with high privacy scores get ACCESS to privacy-preserving services at lower cost

### Step 2: The Futures Contract

- Companies bid on future access to "anonymous aggregates" of privacy-conscious users
- Example: "An insurance company bids $5M for access to 1 million users who opted into health coaching BUT never shared their actual health data"
- The "product" is the PATTERN of people who choose privacy, not their actual data
- Users earn crypto every time their pattern is included in a bid they opted into

### Step 3: The Zero-Knowledge Proof Bridge

- This is the technical magic
- ZK proofs let you PROVE you meet criteria WITHOUT revealing your data
- Example: "I am over 21" — provable without showing your ID
- "I have never had a loan default" — provable without showing your credit report
- "I chose local processing 90% of the time" — provable without showing your queries

### Step 4: The "Surveillance Reversal" Auction

- Every quarter, companies bid on the RIGHT to know less about you
- Like carbon credits, but for privacy
- Company buys "surveillance offset" credits
- Offset = the equivalent of 1,000 people choosing to stay private for a month
- This creates a MARKET for companies to compete on who intrudes LESS

## HOW THIS BEATS THE MONOPOLY

**Google's moat:** They know everything
**Our moat:** We make knowing everything EXPENSIVE

When privacy has a market price:
- Every query you run locally denies a data broker a piece of their future revenue
- That revenue has to come from somewhere — so companies pay you to keep choosing privacy
- The more people choose local AI, the more valuable local AI becomes (better models, more compute, more contributors)
- It's a virtuous cycle that only works if the alternatives are worse

---

## HOW MONEY FLOWS

1. **Data brokers** pay to see aggregates → money goes to USERS who chose privacy
2. **Companies** pay to prove they're not spying → money goes to ZK proof validators
3. **Researchers** pay for anonymized behavioral patterns → money goes to users who opted in
4. **Advertisers** pay to reach privacy-conscious users WITHOUT violating privacy → money goes to users

The key insight: **We're not fighting the surveillance economy. We're pricing it out of existence.**
